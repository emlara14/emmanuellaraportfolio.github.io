Project 2: Feasibility Analysis of Server Virtualization

In this project, I acted as a System Administrator for a fictitious organization, HostPenny, to conduct a comprehensive financial cost-benefit analysis for a large-scale server virtualization initiative[cite: 773, 786, 799].

  • Current State Assessment: I analyzed the operational costs of 20,000 physical servers divided into four functional groups, calculating energy consumption in kWH based on average CPU loads and power rates[cite: 804, 806, 829].

  • Consolidation Strategy: I proposed a 10.2:1 physical consolidation ratio, reducing the hardware footprint to 1,960 high-performance Sun X9-2L servers capable of supporting 3,830 virtual machines[cite: 910, 912, 913].

  • Financial Projections: I developed a 5-year financial projection including hard costs (server purchase, maintenance, and software licenses) and soft costs (administrative effort and backup labor)[cite: 788, 971, 1219, 1222].

  • Dynamic Modeling: I constructed a series of interconnected spreadsheets using shared variables to ensure the feasibility study remained flexible and free of hard-coded numerical values[cite: 1256, 1258, 1260].
